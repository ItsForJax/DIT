How DIT Works

The DIT system is designed to be simple and easy to use, even for beginners. Follow these steps to start managing your finances:

Step 1 — Add Your Debts

Enter your debt details such as the amount borrowed, interest rate, and repayment period in months. You can add up to 5 debts to keep track of multiple loans.

Step 2 — View Your Repayment Schedule

After entering your debt, the system will automatically calculate your monthly payments and generate a repayment checklist. Each month you can mark payments as completed and track your progress.

Step 3 — Input Your Income

Enter your total income (weekly or monthly). This allows the system to help you plan your finances more effectively.

Step 4 — Get Suggested Income Allocation

The system will automatically divide your income using the 50/30/20 rule — 50% to Needs, 30% to Wants, and 20% to Savings. This helps you balance spending and saving habits.

Step 5 — Monitor Your Financial Summary

The dashboard shows your total debt, remaining balance, payment progress, and income allocation. This gives you a clear picture of your financial situation at all times.

Step 6 — Stay Consistent

By regularly updating your records you can avoid missed payments, reduce debt faster, improve financial discipline, and achieve better financial stability.

Amortization Formula

M = P × [r(1+r)n / ((1+r)n − 1)]
P = Principal (amount borrowed)
r = Interest rate (monthly, as decimal)
n = Number of repayments (months)
